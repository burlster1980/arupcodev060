                <h2>Data as the “new utility” – how data powers the world we live in </h2><P>
In today’s world data is integral to our daily lives and we are becoming increasingly reliant on accessing and processing information. This trend means we are looking at data not only as important, but as a fundamental utility. </P><P>
We are all becoming daily users of networks and complex data services, expecting them to be fast and fully functional whenever and wherever we need access. Data centres need to provide the foundational infrastructure to allow the connection or processing of data when we want and without failure. </P><P>
Embracing data as a utility is one of the biggest opportunities for data storage facilities to begin accelerating operational excellence, high-performance and high-efficiency. This focus will see some of the world’s most exciting innovations in data storage and processing.</P>

                <h2>Carbon footprint – increasing size and speed with minimal environmental impact </h2><P>
The key challenge of global digital transformation, connectivity and future of communications technology is the question of energy efficiency. The demand for new computing capabilities, but also the importance of minimal environmental impact seems to conflict. </P><P>
In a recent US study it was found that communications technologies could create up to 3.5% of global emissions by 20201 – surpassing aviation and shipping – and could increase to 14% by 2040. With such rapid rates of increase and so much energy needed to cool and power our data centres we need to explore more sustainable solutions for the dramatic increase in data consumption. </P>
