                <p>In projects large and small, locations near and far, our colleagues are making a real difference, helping some of the world’s most disadvantaged, marginalised and vulnerable people and communities.</p>
                <P>Every Community Engagement project we undertake is a partnership, and we work with a wide range of organisations (ranging from NGOs to academia to industry peers), increasing our impact. </P>

                    <h3 class="h4">Creating microgrids in Dollo Ado, Ethopia</h3>
                    <P class="intro">Around 220,000 refugees live in the Dollo Ado camps in Ehtiopia, having fled droughts in Somalia.</P>
                    <p>Engineers Without Borders (EWB)-USA – in partnership with United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) and IKEA Foundation – asked us to build a team of experts to provide remote technical support for the deployment of renewable energy resources throughout the camps. This key project is now in its second year.</p>
                    <P>For project manager Stephanie Gulliver, this was a chance to help people who have been displaced to get clean, reliable access to power, and teach new skills. “We trained 60 refugees in electrical fundamentals, construction and business, which created paid jobs for them and provided the community with expertise they can call on for future energy projects.</P>
